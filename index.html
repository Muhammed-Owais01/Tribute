Muhammad Ali Jinnah

Founder of a nation

Muhammad Ali Jinnah's Life:

  • 1876 - Born in Karachi
  • 1887 - Admitted to Sind Madrasatul Islam, Karachi
  • 1895 - Became Bar-at-Law
  • 1896 - Returned to Karachi from London
  • 1910 - Elected to the Legislative Assembly, Bombay
  • 1912 - Attended All-India Muslim League Council Meeting
  • 1913 - Joined All-India Muslim League
  • 1916 - Lucknow Pact Signed
  • 1920 - Resigned from the Congress on differences with Gandhi
  • 1927 - Boycotted the Simon Commission
  • 1929 - Jinnah's Fourteen Points
  • 1930 - Attended Round Table Conference in London
  • 1940 - Lahore Resolution Passed
  • 1944 - Jinnah-Gandhi talks
  • 1945 - Participated in Simla Conference
  • 1946 - Direct Action Day
  • 1947 - Pakistan comes into being; the Quaid-e-Azam sworn in as the first Governor-General
  • 1948 - Died in Karachi at the age of 71

"Quaid-i-Azam, Mohammad Ali Jinnah's greatest qualities as a leader were his selflessness, integrity and courage."

--Pakistani Prime Minister Imran Khan
